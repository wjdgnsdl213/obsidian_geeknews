# 24 tips for giving S-tier demos

**원문:** https://newsletter.posthog.com/p/how-to-demo

## 요약
해커톤 데모 50개 이상을 보고 뽑은 S급 데모의 24가지 실전 팁

## 핵심 포인트
- 한 가지 핵심 메시지에 집중하고 빠르게 본론으로 들어가며, 제품 워크스루가 아닌 "피칭"처럼 데모를 진행
- 공감되는 페인포인트로 시작하고 "당신이라면" 화법, Before/After 비교로 임팩트를 키움
- 사전 체크리스트·프리로드/캐싱·실전 연습 같은 준비와, 시각·음향 연출이 데모 완성도를 좌우

## 왜 중요한가
공모전 발표나 포트폴리오 시연, 면접 자기소개에서 결국 "내가 만든 것을 얼마나 설득력 있게 보여주는가"가 평가를 가른다. 기술력만큼 데모/피칭 역량이 중요하다는 것을 보여주는 실전 체크리스트다.

## 내 관심 분야 적용
- 취업/면접: 포트폴리오 프로젝트를 "문제→해결→임팩트" 구조와 Before/After 비교로 설명하면 면접관에게 더 설득력 있게 전달 가능
- 공모전: 발표 자료를 슬라이드 나열이 아닌 "라이브 데모 + 실데이터" 중심으로 구성해 심사위원 임팩트 극대화
- 포트폴리오: 데모 영상/GIF 제작 시 화면 줌·캡션·스토리텔링을 적용해 완성도를 높이기
- 사업: 소상공인 컨설팅 피칭 시 고객의 페인포인트로 시작해 1-스텝 솔루션을 비교 시연하는 구조 활용

## 원문 스크랩

### An engineer's guide on how to demo well

A good demo can be the difference between a project shipping or shutting down, and a startup getting funded or going nowhere.

Yet most developers don't invest in learning how to demo well – probably because we enjoy building things more than presenting them.

I watched 50+ hackathon demos back to back at our offsite last week and, after that many in a row, some patterns became super obvious. Here's a practical list of what set the S-tier demos apart from the rest.

**The basic structure**
1. Pick one main point you want people to remember and make everything else revolve around it.
2. Get to your main point as soon as possible.
3. Treat the demo like a pitch, not a product walkthrough.
4. End with something immediately actionable (QR code, call for contributors, etc).

**Storytelling tactics**
5. Lead with a shared pain point that everyone in the audience understands.
6. Borrow a familiar concept to explain a new one.
7. Create a separate demo app, especially for dev tools.
8. Use "you" framing to put the audience in the right perspective.
9. Demo against the alternative — show the painful current workflow side-by-side with your version.
10. Save how it works for later.

**Setup and delivery**
11. Channel your inner Steve Ballmer — energy matters.
12. Don't apologize ("sorry, it's still rough" lowers expectations).
13. Make it obvious when you're done.
14. Do everything in your power to please the demo gods (demo setup checklist: use a demo project, disable notifications, silence phone, bookmark URL, prep for WiFi failure, zoom browser, test projector).
15. Use real data wherever possible.
16. Pre-load and cache as much as you can.
17. Practice out loud at least once.
18. Don't let perfectionism stop you from demoing.

**Make it fun**
19. Avoid showing plain old code — generate visuals/diagrams instead.
20. Don't just show plain old slides either — active demos win.
21. Default screen recording tools suck — use apps like Screen Studio.
22. Visuals don't need to be beautiful, just need to highlight what's important.
23. Audio is underrated.
24. Do more weird — memorable quirks make demos stick.
